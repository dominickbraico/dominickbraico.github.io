Project Overview

As part of the Illinois Robotics in Space team, I contributed to the design and fabrication of a lunar excavation system for a space exploration rover. This project challenged me to design robust mechanical components capable of operating in simulated lunar conditions while optimizing for weight, durability, and manufacturability. My primary focus was on the belt excavation mechanism, including structural components, custom pulleys, and motor mounting systems.

Illinois Robotics in Space

Illinois Robotics in Space (IRIS) is a student organization at the University of Illinois dedicated to designing and building robots for space exploration competitions. The team participates in the NASA Lunabotics competition, where universities design autonomous robots capable of excavating and transporting lunar regolith (simulated moon soil).

üèÜ Competition Objectives

  • Design and build a lunar excavation robot
  • Excavate maximum amount of simulated regolith within time limit
  • Navigate autonomously in simulated lunar terrain
  • Optimize for weight, efficiency, and reliability
